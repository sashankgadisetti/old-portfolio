Fair Chance for Special Needs

Fair Chance for Special Needs is a afterschool daycare for children with special needs located on the Dublin High School campus. Much of my freshman and sophomore years of high school, I assisted with the running of the program, interacting with the children in addition to other tasks, such as serving food. The children who I communicated with typically had congenital disorders which impaired their ability to learn and communicated, such as autism and down syndrome, making it harder for their parents to manage a balanced life. Interacting with them allowed me to remove any stigma I had around children with special needs and helped me gain a better understanding of how drastically a medical condition can affect a person and their families. I have also volunteered at the annual Night to Shine event hosted by the Tim Tebot Foundation, a prom-like experience for children with special needs, and a relaxing night for their parents.

PROJECTS & INFLUENTIAL GUEST SPEAKERS


Dublin High Science Fair 2019


I worked as a member of a team of 3 to test the presence of heavy metals, which can be toxic in large quantities, in household objects.

Heavy metals are those which are poisonous to the human body after ingestion or prolonged exposure. These metals have the potential to be deadly and people should be aware of their effects and presence in household objects. These toxic metals are typically favored due to their low cost and low melting point, making them easier to mass produce. We used water testing strips for poisonous metals in order to test for these metals after we dissolved a sanded powder from these objects into the water.

Grand Rounds 2020


I worked as a part of a 3 person team to reasearch the SARS outbreak in 2002. Overall, it was a great experience which allowed me to both learn a large amount about the spread of dangerous pathogens and also helped me with my presentational skills.
